High School Graduation

This project explores the public high school graduation rates in the United States for the 2015-2016 school year in an attempt to better understand what factors (such as race or economic status) influence whether or not students complete their high school education.

How Rates Were Calculated

Schools report the exact number of students that fall into certain demographics (such as "economically disadvantaged") but they are not required to report the exact number of students from that group that graduate. Instead, they can report a range such as "85%-89%"" or "Less than 50%". In cases where there are fewer than ten students in a group, the percentage is not reported at all to protect the privacy of the students. To estimate the graduation rates, I used the middle value in each reported range and omitted the students where no range was given.
